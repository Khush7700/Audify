Compliance Isn’t Optional

ADA Lawsuits Are on the Rise. Is Your Business Protected?

ADA and WCAG accessibility standards aren’t just best practices — they’re legal requirements. Thousands of online businesses are being sued each year for having websites that aren't accessible to users with disabilities.

Costly Lawsuits & Settlements

Thousands of businesses are sued each year for inaccessible websites and many settle out of court to avoid bigger losses. It’s not a matter of if, but when.

Legal Fines & Penalties

Failing to meet ADA/WCAG standards can lead to $75,000+ in fines for first-time violations under federal law and more for repeated offenses.

Inaccessible Sites Lose Customers Instantly

40% of users with disabilities will abandon a site if they encounter accessibility issues. That’s money left on the table — every single day.

Missed Revenue from 1 in 4 Americans with Disabilities

Roughly 26% of U.S. adults live with a disability, and this number is expected to grow with aging populations. Accessibility is not a niche concern.

Bad for SEO & Search Rankings

Google favors accessible websites. ADA-friendly sites tend to have better structure, which improves SEO performance and page experience scores.

Your Plugins May Not Be Enough

Automated overlays and plugins often fail to meet true compliance — and in some cases, make the experience worse. Courts have ruled against sites relying only on them.

Do I really need ADA compliance for my website?

Yes. If your business has a website and serves the public, you're legally expected to be accessible. ADA lawsuits are increasing yearly—and even small brands aren't spared.

How can ADA compliance help my business beyond avoiding lawsuits?

It boosts your SEO, improves user experience, and strengthens brand trust. Plus, with 26% of U.S. adults living with a disability, making your site accessible gives you a clear edge over competitors whose sites they can't use.

What happens if I ignore ADA compliance?

You risk lawsuits, fines up to $75,000 (or more), legal fees, and loss of credibility. It only takes one user filing a claim to disrupt your business.

What exactly does Audify do?

We audit your website for accessibility issues, fix them (remediation), and ensure compliance with WCAG 2.1 standards. We also provide monitoring, reports, and maintenance

How long does it take to make my site compliant?

Most websites can be audited and remediated within 3 to 4 weeks. The timeline depends on site size and complexity. We will provide you with a clear timeline after initial review.

Is ADA compliance just about screen readers?

Not at all. We ensure your site works with keyboard navigation, color contrast standards, alt text, forms, videos, and more. It’s about total usability for all.
